Research Interests

Climate extreme events, hydrological extremes, statistical modeling, physical understanding, ecological effects

Research Profile

  • Identification and Ecological Impacts of Extreme Climate Events: Developed a new statistical method to identify extreme and independent climate events on a daily scale and across four seasons; and explored the association between four types of extremes (droughts, pluvials, heatwaves, and coldwaves) and butterfly abundance in England.
  • Investigation of Compound Climatic Events: Focusing on compound events (combinations of multiple climatic drivers and/or hazards that occur simultaneously or successively and cause societal or environmental impacts) we examined various types of compound events and quantified their effects on crop yield considering different stages of crop growth.
  • Optimization of Agricultural Water Use in Arid Regions: Employed various statistical methods such as Vine copula, EEMD, machine learning, and uncertainty representation methods for better modeling the variables in agricultural water allocation in arid regions.
